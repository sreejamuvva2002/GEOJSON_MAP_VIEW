## Rules Used

1. **Usable latitude/longitude**: both numeric and within valid Earth ranges (`-90 <= lat <= 90`, `-180 <= lon <= 180`).
2. **Computed county**: the Georgia county polygon that contains the point. This is a standard point-in-polygon rule and is the correct rule for county membership.
3. **Outside Georgia county polygon**: point does not fall inside any county polygon in `Counties_Georgia.geojson`.
4. **County mismatch**: the workbook's stated county extracted from `Location` does not equal the polygon-derived `computed_county`.
5. **Address state conflict**: the address ends in a non-`GA` state abbreviation. This is weaker than polygon evidence because mailing/HQ addresses can differ, but it is still a valid inconsistency signal in a Georgia facility workbook.
6. **Strong location/address conflict**: parsed city from `Location` disagrees with parsed city from `Address`, with neither string containing the other. This is a heuristic signal, not a geometry proof.
7. **Duplicate coordinate cluster**: exact same `(latitude, longitude)` is assigned to more than one row. This is suspicious, especially when it spans multiple company names, but not by itself conclusive.

## Duplicate-Coordinate Re-check

The first-pass audit reported exact duplicate coordinate clusters. That signal needs qualification because some companies legitimately appear multiple times for multiple EV roles or alias names.

- Exact duplicate coordinate clusters among usable-coordinate rows: `17`
- `6` clusters are **same-company-only** duplicates. These are often benign repeated rows, multi-role rows, or name variants.
- `7` clusters are **shared-address or alias** duplicates. These may reflect the same campus, the same physical address under different names, or address-level reuse.
- `4` clusters are **suspicious multi-company, multi-address exact-coordinate reuse**. These are stronger signs of copied or reused coordinates because different addresses share the exact same point.

To test whether duplicate rows were inflating the bad-news metrics, the workbook was re-evaluated at a more conservative **facility level** using the unique key `(Company, Address, Latitude, Longitude)`.

- Unique facilities with usable coordinates: `194`
- Unique facilities outside Georgia county polygons: `9` (`4.64%`)
- Unique facilities with county label and usable coordinates: `173`
- Unique-facility county matches: `10`
- Unique-facility county mismatches: `156`
- Unique-facility county mismatch rate: `90.17%`

Only `4` unique facilities have multiple EV roles attached to the same company/address/coordinate, and only `2` of those facilities even have a county label that participates in the county-match test. This means the multi-role duplication issue is real but **too small to explain the county mismatch pattern**.

An even stricter test is to remove **all** facility rows that participate in any exact duplicate-coordinate cluster, including alias-style and shared-campus cases. On the remaining non-duplicate, county-labeled facilities:

- Non-duplicate county matches: `8`
- Non-duplicate county mismatches: `136`
- Non-duplicate county mismatch rate: `94.44%`

So the geo-integrity problem remains overwhelming even after removing the entire duplicate-coordinate subset from consideration.

## Major Conclusions With Basis

### Conclusion 1: The problem is systematic, not a small set of bad rows

- **Rule used**: polygon containment plus county label comparison.
- **Why valid**: if coordinates are correct, most Georgia facility points should fall inside their stated county polygon.
- **Supporting evidence**: `162` mismatches out of `179` county-labeled rows with usable coordinates (`90.50%`).
- **Interpretation**: that rate is far too high to be explained by a handful of bad geocodes. Re-checking at the unique-facility level still leaves `156` mismatches out of `173` county-labeled facilities (`90.17%`).

### Conclusion 2: The evidence suggests workbook contamination or an upstream bad join, not just isolated geocoding noise

- **Rule used**: compare company name, labeled `Location`, `Address`, and polygon-derived county together.
- **Why valid**: if a row says one Georgia place but the address names a different city/state, that contradiction is inside the workbook itself.
- **Supporting evidence**:
  - `ACM Georgia LLC`: `Calhoun, Gordon County` vs address in `Warrenton, GA`
  - `Adient`: `Ringgold, Catoosa County` vs address in `West Point, GA`
  - `Elan Technology Inc.`: `Atlanta, Fulton County` vs address in `Midway, GA`
  - `Michelin Tread Technologies`: `Lawrenceville, Gwinnett County` vs address in `Greenville, SC`
- **Interpretation**: these are strong signs of misaligned rows, contaminated workbook content, or mixed facility/HQ records. The contradiction exists before the repo's own polygon logic is applied.

### Conclusion 3: The current geo layer cannot be trusted for county analytics

- **Rule used**: county analytics require correct county membership.
- **Why valid**: if the point is outside the stated county, county counts and county gap analytics become wrong.
- **Supporting evidence**: `162` county mismatches and `9` rows outside all Georgia county polygons.

### Conclusion 4: The current geo layer cannot be trusted for distance/radius search

- **Rule used**: distance search assumes the stored point location is the actual facility point.
- **Why valid**: point-to-point or point-to-polygon distance is only as good as the point itself.
- **Supporting evidence**: rows with out-of-state addresses, rows outside Georgia polygons, and rows whose computed county disagrees with the labeled county.

### Conclusion 5: Ingestion should fail fast

- **Rule used**: compare observed rates against the stated thresholds.
- **Why valid**: letting ingestion proceed would silently contaminate the map, analytics, and retrieval layers.
- **Supporting evidence**: outside-GA rate `4.46%` and county mismatch rate `90.50%`, both above the requested thresholds.

## Supported Interpretation

### Is this a small number of bad rows, or systematic?

Systematic. The strongest evidence is the county mismatch rate: `90.50%` (`162` of `179`).

### Does the evidence suggest a bad join between company rows and coordinate rows?

Probably yes, but with one important caveat: the contradictions are already visible inside the workbook itself. That means the problem may be an upstream join, workbook contamination, or mixed facility/HQ records. The current audit supports the claim that the coordinate source is unreliable; it cannot by itself prove which upstream step introduced the corruption.

### Can this geo layer be trusted for map visualization?

Not for professor-defensible geographic visualization. A map can still be drawn, but the evidence does not support trusting the points as facility-accurate.

### Can it be trusted for county-level analytics?

No. County-level analytics depend on correct county membership, which the audit shows is violated at a very high rate.

### Can it be trusted for distance/radius search?

No. Distance/radius search requires trustworthy point geometry, and the current point layer fails polygon and consistency checks.

### Should ingestion fail-fast?

Yes. Both requested fail-fast thresholds are exceeded.

## Uncertainty and Assumptions

- The polygon containment logic assumes `Counties_Georgia.geojson` is the authoritative county boundary source for Georgia.
- The county comparison assumes the `Location` field is intended to describe the facility county. Rows like `Georgia` or rows without a parsable county are excluded from the county-mismatch denominator.
- Address-based conflict rules are weaker than polygon evidence because an address can refer to HQ, mailing, or another corporate site. That is why the strongest claims in this report rely on polygon containment and county mismatch, not just address text.
- Exact duplicate coordinates across different companies are suspicious but not always wrong; industrial parks and shared campuses can produce legitimate duplicates. They are therefore presented as evidence to inspect, not as standalone proof of corruption.

## Final Verdict

**Verdict: not trustworthy / ingestion should fail**

The geometry evidence is strong enough that this workbook should not pass ingestion into a county-aware map, county analytics layer, or distance/radius search system without a coordinate-join audit and corrective cleanup.
